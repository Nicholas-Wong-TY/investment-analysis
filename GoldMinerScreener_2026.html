Investment Thesis
Wheaton Precious Metals is not technically a miner — it's a streaming company that pays upfront capital to miners in exchange for the right to purchase future gold and silver at fixed low prices (~$400–$700/oz). This means Wheaton carries NONE of the operational risks that make mining stocks dangerous: no cartel exposure, no government seizures, no cost inflation blowouts, no tailings disasters, no pit accidents. The business model generates margins that would be impossible in physical mining. At $5,000 gold, Wheaton earns ~$4,300–$4,400 per oz while its "cost" is simply the streaming contract fixed rate. Record Q3 2025 revenue of $487.7M was up 77% year over year with near-zero capex requirements. WPM has 15+ new streams in the pipeline for 2026–2027, meaning its production profile — already ~800,000 gold-equivalent oz — is set to grow without any new capital deployment at the operator level. The P/E looks elevated (~28–30×) but on a P/FCF or EV/EBITDA basis it is reasonable for the best-in-class franchise in precious metals. The stock is 24% below the average analyst price target. UBS raised their target to $270 — implying 50%+ upside from current levels. If gold reaches $6,000+, Wheaton's earnings would surge disproportionately relative to any physical miner.
